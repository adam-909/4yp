Choosing the method and parameters for the graph construction is a non-trivial task yet crucial to

6.3. Training Details
All deep learning models were developed in Python 3.8 using the Keras API of the TensorFlow [54]
machine learning platform. Due to time and computation constraints, a single training and testing
window was chosen. Data from 2011 to 2016 formed the training/validation set, with data from 2017
2023 used for testing. A long test horizon in comparison to train/validation horizon was chosen to
reduce the variance of different volatility regimes between years in the testing window in order to ob
tain a more conclusive measure of each model’s performance. The limitations of this approach are
discussed in Section 8.1. The test window notably contains the spike in market-wide volatility in March
2020, resulting from the COVID-19 pandemic. Within the sample window, a train validation split of
80%–20% was used. Consistent with [33], [35], and other previous works, the gradients for back
propagation were calculated using minibatch stochastic gradient descent with the Adam optimiser,
and early stopping with a patience of 25 epochs based on validation loss was used to prevent over
fitting. Hyperparameter optimisation was performed with 50 iterations of random search. The full list
of parameters and hyperparameters can be seen in Appendix A.2. Model calibration was performed
on a local machine with a NVIDIA GeForce RTX 2070S GPU and AMD Ryzen 5 3600X CPU.

6.4. Performance Metrics
When evaluating a trading strategy, it is typical to consider three broad categories of performance
metrics: profitability measures, risk measures, and overall performanceratios. Togethertheseprovide
a multifaceted assessment of the robustness of a trading strategy, balancing the trade-off between
return generation and risk management.
Profitability: the expected return, and hit rate (the percentage of days with positive returns across
the test period). A hit rate exceeding roughly 55% is considered attractive.
Risk: annualisedvolatility, downside deviation (DD, volatility of negative returns), and maximum draw
down (MDD, greatest observed loss from a peak to a trough over a specific time frame).
Performance: Sharpe ratio (Equation (48)), Sortino ratio (expected return/DD), Calmar ratio (ex
pected return/MDD), and average profit/average loss. A Sharpe greater than 1 reflects a strategy
with modest profitability, greater than 2 is very good, and greater than 3 is considered excellent. A
Sortino ratio between 2-3, and Calmar ratio of 1-3, indicate good expected returns relative to observed
